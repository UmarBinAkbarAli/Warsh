# Noor — Mission & Product Vision

## App Name
**Noor** *(نُور)* — meaning Light, Guidance, Clarity.

- Play Store listing: **Noor — Learn Arabic**
- Short name: **Noor** (icon, verbal)
- Internal/dev name: **ArabAI** (repo, internal docs only)

---

## Mission
Make Quranic Arabic accessible to every Muslim — regardless of background, budget, or how busy their life is.

## Vision
A world where every Muslim can hear the Quran and understand every word directly, without translation.

---

## Who We're Building For
Pakistani, Indian, and wider Muslim audience. Someone who:
- Recites the Quran daily but doesn't understand it
- Has tried and quit Duolingo (felt too gamey, no Islamic context)
- Doesn't have time for a madrasa class
- Trusts a warm teacher more than a cold productivity app

**Primary goal:** understand the Quran. Secondary: travel, study, general Arabic.

---

## Core Values
| Value | In practice |
|---|---|
| Warmth | Feels like a teacher who believes in you |
| Clarity | Makes Arabic manageable, one step at a time |
| Depth | Builds real understanding — not dumbed down |
| Belonging | Made for us — Pakistani, Indian, Muslim, learner |
| Progress | Every day you open the app, you're closer |

---

## AI Tutor Persona — Ustaad Noor
The AI character embedded in the chat tab.

- Warm, patient, never condescending
- Gets excited when you understand something
- Feels like a 35-year-old scholar who studied in Madinah but grew up in Lahore
- Available at 2am, endlessly patient
- **NOT** a Silicon Valley productivity app. Not a cold AI chatbot. Not a guilty-trip madrasa.

---

## Product Model
- Mobile-first (Android, React Native / Expo)
- Structured lesson curriculum (chapters → lessons) with chapter locking
- Placement quiz to skip known content
- XP + streak + gems gamification layer
- AI chat with daily message limit (5/day in v1)
- Achievement system (schema ready, UI pending)

---

## Lesson Flow (VOCABULARY type — flagship)
A 5-beat structure per lesson:
1. **Hook** — Quranic ayah raises curiosity ("what does this mean?")
2. **Discover** — vocabulary cards with Arabic, translation, transliteration
3. **Practice** — interactive exercises (TRUE_FALSE, TAP_TRANSLATION, FILL_BLANK, BUILD_SENTENCE)
4. **Reveal** — learner sees the ayah again, now understanding it
5. **Close** — XP earned, Ustaad Noor tip, streak update

Legacy lesson types (FLASHCARD, FILL_BLANK, MULTIPLE_CHOICE, MATCHING, LISTENING) use a simpler single-screen flow.
