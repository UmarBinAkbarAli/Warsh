### The three pedagogical peaks

Three chapter clusters have been identified as significantly harder than baseline and require expanded depth:

- **Peak 1: Chapters 34–36 (Book 4 Lesson 1 expanded)** — المضارع, future tense with سَ, and المصدر. The single biggest conceptual jump in the curriculum. Expands from 1 Madinah lesson to 3 Warsh chapters.
- **Peak 2: Chapters 55–57 (Book 6 Lessons 1–2 expanded)** — الإعراب والبناء, الأسماء الخمسة, weak verbs, الأفعال الخمسة. The grammar-science apex. Expands from 2 Madinah lessons to 3 Warsh chapters with 8–9 lessons each.
- **Peak 3: Chapters 67–72 (Book 8 rhetorical lessons)** — الحال, التمييز, الاستثناء, المنادى, لو. Each gets generous lesson depth because these are the structures that make the Quran *feel* like the Quran.

## Part D — Book 2 Chapter Mapping (Chapters 11–23)

**Book 2 focus:** Structural expansion. The learner moves from "naming things" to "describing relationships between things." Plurals, family/possession, relative pronouns, attached pronouns. Past tense recognition begins (though formal conjugation comes later).

### Chapter 11: The Home and Family
- **Madinah source:** Book 2, Lesson 1 (البيت والأسرة)
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Masad (continuing from Chapter 10), An-Nasr
- **Concepts:** فِيهِ / فِيهَا (in it — masc/fem), possession with simple noun pairs, family vocabulary
- **Key vocabulary:** أُمّ، أَب، أَخ، أُخْت، اِبْن، بِنْت، أُسْرَة
- **Notes:** First introduction to feminine forms in earnest. Light, warm chapter after Book 1's intensity.

### Chapter 12: Introductions & Personal Questions
- **Madinah source:** Book 2, Lesson 2 (التعارف والأسئلة الشخصية)
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** An-Nasr
- **Concepts:** Asking name/nationality/profession, **first exposure to past-tense verbs (ذَهَبَ، ذَهَبَتْ)**, feminine forms expanded
- **Key vocabulary:** اِسْم، جِنْسِيَّة، مِهْنَة، ذَهَبَ، رَجَعَ
- **Notes:** Past-tense verbs appear here as recognition only — formal conjugation deferred to Books 4–5. Treat as vocabulary.

**SP3 — Classroom and Halaqa Phrases (after Chapter 12)**

### Chapter 13: Plural Forms — An Introduction
- **Madinah source:** Book 2, Lesson 3 (الجمع والصفات) — Part 1
- **Lessons:** 6 *(expanded — plurals are a known difficulty spike)*
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Kafirun (الْكَافِرُونَ requires plural understanding)
- **Concepts:** Sound masculine plural (كَاتِبُونَ), sound feminine plural (كَاتِبَات), introduction to broken plurals as a *recognition concept* (don't try to systematize broken plurals yet)
- **Key vocabulary:** مُسْلِمُون، مُؤْمِنُون، كَافِرُون، طُلَّاب، أَوْلَاد، بُيُوت
- **Notes:** Broken plurals are conceptually hard. Teach them here as "Arabic has a third plural type that we learn word-by-word" rather than as a rule system. Full broken plural patterns come in Book 6.

### Chapter 14: Describing Plurals
- **Madinah source:** Book 2, Lesson 3 (الجمع والصفات) — Part 2
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Kafirun, Al-Ma'un
- **Concepts:** Plural noun + plural adjective agreement, non-human plurals taking feminine singular adjectives (an important quirk: الْكُتُب جَدِيدَة)
- **Key vocabulary:** كَبِيرَة (modifying plural), جَدِيدَة، صَغِيرَة، جَمِيلَة
- **Notes:** The non-human plural rule is unusual for English speakers. Use multiple Discover cards to scaffold it.

### Chapter 15: Demonstratives Expanded — هَؤُلَاء، أُولَئِك
- **Madinah source:** Book 2, Lesson 4 (الإشارة والمواقع)
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Ma'un, Al-Kawthar
- **Concepts:** Plural demonstratives هَؤُلَاء (these), أُولَئِك (those), positional vocabulary
- **Key vocabulary:** هَؤُلَاء، أُولَئِك، أَمَام، خَلْف، فَوْق، تَحْت
- **Notes:** Quranically, أُولَئِك is enormous — it appears in dozens of ayat about believers/disbelievers. Strong Reveal beat material.

**R3 — Mid-Book 2 Review (after Chapter 15)**

### Chapter 16: School and Students
- **Madinah source:** Book 2, Lesson 5 (المدرسة والطلاب)

### Chapter 19: Attached Pronouns — Possession (Singular)

### Chapter 20: Attached Pronouns — Plural Forms

- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Falaq, completing toward Al-Ikhlas

## Part E — Book 3 Chapter Mapping (Chapters 24–33)

**Book 3 focus:** The great consolidators. إِنَّ and لَيْسَ — two particles that transform the learner's ability to read the Quran. By the end of Book 3, the learner can affirmatively assert ("Truly, X is Y") and negate ("X is not Y") in nominal sentences — the most common Quranic sentence type.

### Chapter 24: Conversational Foundations & إِنَّ
- **Madinah source:** Book 3, Lesson 1 (Introduction + إِنَّ)
- **Lessons:** 6 *(expanded — إِنَّ is Quranically explosive)*
- **Tadabbur focus:** **Al-Kawthar (إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ الْكَوْثَر)**
- **Concepts:** The particle إِنَّ (truly/indeed), its sister particles (overview), interrogatives review, basic numbers, descriptive vocabulary
- **Key vocabulary:** إِنَّ، إِنَّا (إِنَّ + نَا), أَعْطَى، الْكَوْثَر، numbers 1–10
- **Notes:** This chapter should culminate in the learner parsing إِنَّا أَعْطَيْنَاكَ الْكَوْثَر — a powerful Tadabbur moment. The Reveal beat should make this explicit.

### Chapter 25: لَيْسَ — Negating the Nominal Sentence
- **Madinah source:** Book 3, Lesson 2 (Negation with لَيْسَ)
- **Lessons:** 6 *(expanded — Quranically essential)*
- **Tadabbur focus:** **Al-Ikhlas (لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ — partial; لَمْ comes in Book 5, but the negation logic starts here)**
- **Concepts:** لَيْسَ in all forms (لَيْسَ، لَيْسَتْ، لَيْسُوا), negative conversational responses, sentence transformations
- **Key vocabulary:** لَيْسَ، لَيْسَتْ، كُفُو، أَحَد
- **Notes:** Combined with Chapter 24's إِنَّ, the learner now has both poles of nominal assertion.

## Part F — Book 4 Chapter Mapping (Chapters 34–43)

**Book 4 focus: PEAK 1 — Time enters the language.** The learner crosses the threshold from "understanding sentences" to "understanding action in time." This is where Al-Fatiha's Tadabbur fully completes.

### Chapter 34: The Present Tense (المضارع) — Introduction
- **Madinah source:** Book 4, Lesson 1 (المضارع) — Part 1 of 3
- **Lessons:** 7 *(heavily expanded — the single biggest grammatical leap)*
- **Tadabbur focus:** **Al-Fatiha — full completion (إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِين)** — *Tadabbur unlock #1, the most emotionally powerful moment in the app*
- **Concepts:** What المضارع is (ongoing/present/habitual action), the prefix system (يَ، تَ، نَ، أَ), basic conjugation in indicative mode (مرفوع)
- **Key vocabulary:** يَعْبُد، نَعْبُد، نَسْتَعِين، يَفْعَل، يَقُول، يَعْلَم
- **Template note:** **First VERB_PATTERN lesson in the curriculum.** Lesson 2 of this chapter should use VERB_PATTERN to show the full present-tense table for a single verb.
- **Notes:** This is the chapter that justifies the entire curriculum. The Reveal beat should be Al-Fatiha 1:5 (إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِين) — a moment where the learner suddenly understands what they have been saying in salah for years. The M6 milestone (Al-Fatiha understood) fires here. Push notification, badge, special Noor message.

### Chapter 35: The Future Tense (سَ and سَوْفَ)
- **Madinah source:** Book 4, Lesson 1 (المضارع) — Part 2 of 3
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Begins Al-Ma'un preparation
- **Concepts:** Future tense marker سَ (prefixed) and سَوْفَ (separate), distinction between the two (سَ = near future; سَوْفَ = more distant or emphatic)
- **Key vocabulary:** سَنُعِيد، سَيَقُول، سَيَجْعَل، سَوْفَ تَعْلَم
- **Notes:** Small concept, big Quranic frequency. Especially common in warning/promise ayat.

### Chapter 36: The Verbal Noun (المصدر)
- **Madinah source:** Book 4, Lesson 1 (المضارع) — Part 3 of 3
- **Lessons:** 6 *(expanded — conceptually difficult for English speakers)*
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Ma'un
- **Concepts:** The masdar — "the act of doing" as a noun (الذِّكْر، الصَّلَاة، الْحَمْد، الْعِبَادَة), recognition rather than full derivation (full pattern in Book 5)
- **Key vocabulary:** ذِكْر، صَلَاة، حَمْد، عِبَادَة، اِسْتِغْفَار، إِيمَان
- **Notes:** No clean English parallel. Use heavy visual scaffolding — Discover cards should show the verb-noun-masdar triangle clearly (ذَكَرَ → يَذْكُر → ذِكْر). Expand Discover beats.

**R8 — Post-Peak 1 Reinforcement (after Chapter 36)**

### Chapter 37: Feminine Verb Forms (Present Tense)
- **Madinah source:** Book 4, Lesson 2 (Feminine Verb Forms)
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Al-Ma'un consolidation
- **Concepts:** Feminine present-tense conjugation (تَفْعَل for she), feminine plural (يَفْعَلْن), addressing females (تَفْعَلِين)
- **Key vocabulary:** Verbs from Chapter 34 in feminine forms
- **Notes:** Important for parsing references to Maryam, feminine plurals (مُؤْمِنَات), and grammatically feminine nouns (السَّمَاء، الأَرْض، الشَّمْس).

### Chapter 38: Expanded Verb Usage and Communication
- **Madinah source:** Book 4, Lesson 3 (Expanded Verb Usage)
- **Lessons:** 5
- **Tadabbur focus:** Toward Quraysh
- **Concepts:** Longer verb-rich dialogues, verb transformations in context
- **Notes:** Applied chapter. Heavy use of CONVERSATION_BUILDER.

## Part N — Open Questions for Future Decision

These are conscious deferrals — questions raised by the mapping work that don't need answering now but will need to be addressed before launch:

1. **Should Book 6's formal i'rab notation be optional vs required?** The "Scholar's Path branching" idea was floated and provisionally rejected because Book 7's applied spiral does the integration work. But if user testing shows Chapter 55 is a high-drop-off point, a "skip detailed i'rab notation" toggle might be worth adding.

2. **Should there be an "alphabet & tajweed primer" pre-Chapter 1?** The current spec assumes the learner can read Arabic letters (most South Asian Muslims can, having learned Qaida). If beta testing reveals this assumption is wrong, an optional pre-curriculum module may be needed.

3. **How granular should milestone celebrations be?** Currently M6 fires for each Tadabbur Surah unlock. With 11 Surahs unlocking across 5 books, that's a lot of celebration moments. Worth user-testing whether celebration fatigue sets in.

4. **Audio production strategy for SPOKEN_PHRASES lessons in Books 7–8.** SP10 (Hajj du'a) and SP11 (capstone) deserve human Fus'ha voice rather than TTS. Sourcing decision pending.

5. **Phase 3 Surah progression beyond Al-Fil.** Working assumption: continue backwards through Juz 30 (Al-Humazah → At-Takathur → Al-Qariah → Al-Adiyat → ...). To be locked when Phase 3 development begins.
